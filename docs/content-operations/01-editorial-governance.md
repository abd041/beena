# Editorial Governance

## Purpose

All BEEÑA-E content must read as **senior ophthalmic biotech advisory** — not startup marketing, not generic AI thought leadership, and not consumer health blogging.

## Tone of voice

| Attribute | Do | Don't |
|-----------|----|-------|
| Register | Consultative, measured, precise | Hype, superlatives, urgency tricks |
| Audience | Biotech executives, regulatory leads, medical affairs | General public, patient-facing advocacy (except RISE) |
| Evidence | Precedents, endpoints, agency paths | Vague “innovation” without mechanism |
| Claims | Qualified, defensible | Guarantees, “best,” “world-class” |

## Content types

### Insights / articles

- Executive thought leadership on development, regulation, access, or commercialization
- Minimum depth: **900+ words**, **4+ `##` sections** when published (*enforced in CMS*)
- See [Article framework](./02-article-framework.md)

### Portfolio / case studies

- Representative engagements (anonymized clients acceptable)
- Challenge → approach → results with operational and regulatory detail
- See [Case study standards](./03-case-study-standards.md)

### Services

- Service pages describe **what sponsors receive**, not aspirational brand poetry
- Updates require scientific/regulatory review if claims change

### Homepage

- Copy changes are **change-controlled** (not casual CMS edits)
- Must align with fixed section structure and brand system

### RISE Pakistan Health

- Warmer, community-oriented; still professional
- See [RISE governance](./08-rise-pakistan-health.md)

## Paragraph structure

- **One idea per paragraph**; 2–4 sentences typical
- Lead with the decision or implication, then supporting detail
- Avoid bullet-only articles; use bullets only for takeaways or recommendations

## Heading hierarchy

- Article body uses `## Section title` (renders as H2 on site)
- Page hero provides the H1 — **do not** duplicate the title as `##` in body
- Do not skip levels or use H2 for single-line callouts

## CTAs

Approved patterns:

- Schedule a Consultation
- Discuss strategic priorities
- Contact Our Team
- Explore collaboration (sparingly)

Avoid: “Get started,” “Unlock,” “Book a demo,” “Join the revolution.”

## Citations & credibility

- Reference **FDA, EMA, HTA, or published precedents** where claims are non-obvious
- Do not invent statistics; use ranges or qualitative framing if data is confidential
- Distinguish **illustrative** portfolio content from live client claims

## Metadata rules

- Every published URL has unique title, excerpt, and meta description
- See [SEO content system](./04-seo-content-system.md)

## Review workflow

1. Author drafts in CMS (status: **draft**)
2. Self-check against [Pre-publish checklist](./09-pre-publish-checklist.md)
3. Second reviewer for published tier (scientific or regulatory reviewer when topic requires)
4. Publish only when **Publish readiness** shows no blocking issues
